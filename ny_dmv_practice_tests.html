What Is the NY DMV Written Test?

The New York DMV written test (also called the knowledge test or permit test) is a required exam for anyone applying for a new driver's license or learner's permit in New York State. The test consists of 20 multiple-choice questions drawn from the NY Driver's Manual, and you must answer at least 14 correctly (70%) to pass.

The test covers traffic laws, road signs, right-of-way rules, speed limits, safe driving practices, and New York-specific regulations. Failing the test means you must wait and retake it — so thorough preparation is essential.

NY DMV Written Test — Key Facts

The NY DMV knowledge test is administered at DMV offices across New York State. You must bring proper identification and pay the applicable fee. The test is available in multiple languages. If you fail, you must wait before retaking it. First-time test takers who want to get a learner's permit must also complete a pre-licensing course before the road test.
